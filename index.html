This document defines the official standards, entitlements, and roadmap for all technology assets across Wahl Clipper Corporation globally. It serves as the authoritative reference for IT procurement, deployment, security compliance, and infrastructure planning.

01
Classification Framework
User Tiers & Entitlements
All technology entitlements at Wahl Clipper Corporation are governed by a four-tier classification system. Each employee is assigned a tier at the time of onboarding, based on their role, responsibilities, and workload profile. Tier upgrades require written approval from the employee's department head and the IT Director. The tiers below represent baseline entitlements — additional items may be approved on a case-by-case basis through the standard procurement process.

Asset Policy
Hardware Lifecycle & Refresh Schedule
1
Procurement
Approved requisition, vendor order, asset tagging
2
Deployment
MDM enrollment, OS config, app push, user handover
3
In-Service
Monitoring, patching, warranty support, helpdesk
4
Refresh Eval.
Performance review, upgrade eligibility assessment
5
Retirement
Secure wipe (NIST 800-88), certified disposal or donation
⚠️
Early refresh requests require written justification from the line manager and are subject to IT Director approval. Accidental damage outside of manufacturer warranty is assessed individually — the company covers one incident per device per cycle; subsequent incidents may require employee cost-sharing.
07
Compliance & Governance
Security Standards
All Wahl Clipper Corporation devices must comply with the following security baseline regardless of tier. Exceptions require written authorization from the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO). Compliance is verified quarterly via automated MDM reporting.
08
Standard Configuration
Approved Software Stack
ℹ️
Software not on the approved list requires a Software Request submitted via the IT Service Portal. Requests are evaluated for security compatibility, license cost, and duplication of existing tools. Shadow IT (unauthorized software installations) is a policy violation and subject to disciplinary action.
